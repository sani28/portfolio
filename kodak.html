Kodak Experience Design

  Kodak Annex is a conceptual tablet application that was designed in homage to Kodak's historical mark as a brand. Intended for film enthusiasts to reconnect with the unique analogue experience that Kodak created, the tangibility of the tablet feature designs offers an adaption to evolved technology that re-highlights the heart of Kodak: Analogue.

Challenge

  The challange of Kodak's business problem was to rebuild a demand for film again in a withering analogue world. We recognized the core heart of Kodak's brand in analog and wanted to acknowledge the niche market of independent filmmakers/film enthusiasts that we could build new and enticing experiences for.

pdf

Limitations

  Addressing the high price range in making film into a hobby was one of the main issues we wanted to ovecome by allowing users to shop by a budget. However due time constraints, we chose to have quality over the quantity of features and decided to omit this in our final design. Although we couldn't complete a budgeting filter as one of desired touchpoint feature, I learned that our work flow efficiency could of have been better improved by stricter deadines. I also learned that we could of made better key decisions throughout the process of our design iterations rather than prolonging redundant group ideation.

Solution

  Our final design featured three main ideas: direct interactions in learning how to use a film camera, inspirational content, and an educational showcase on the variety of aesthetic films. Overall, we created a digital platform designed for film enthusiasts to learn more about film and promoted it in way that aimed to entice interest and encouraged the ease of use.

My Role

  In this project my roles included: leading team on tight schedules, ideating the business pitch, copywritting, and the making of visual graphics/presentation slides.
